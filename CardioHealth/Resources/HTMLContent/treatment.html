The treatment of heart attacks and strokes centers around restoring blood flow to the heart and brain, respectively, if not too much time has passed. For heart attacks, this is typically done with a catheter that can inflate a balloon to open the blockage in the coronary artery and leave a metal stent to keep the artery open. For most strokes, a powerful blood thinner can be given to dissolve any blood clot contributing to the blockage and restore blood flow.
